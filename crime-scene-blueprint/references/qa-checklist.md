# QA Checklist

Use this reference to review or repair a scenario.

## Solvability

- The culprit can be identified before reveal using public evidence.
- At least two independent proof paths converge on the culprit.
- The decisive evidence is not the only clue that matters.
- The final clue removes ambiguity rather than introducing an unsupported answer.

## Fairness

- Critical clues are visible before the accusation slide.
- No answer depends on outside knowledge unless explicitly designed as an expert puzzle.
- Red herrings are explainable after reveal.
- Players can distinguish "suspicious" from "proven" through evidence.

## Alibi Integrity

- Every suspect has a claimed alibi and a real alibi.
- Every false alibi has a discoverable contradiction.
- Every innocent suspect has a plausible reason for lying or omitting something.
- Alibis are cross-checked by at least one physical clue, one time clue, and one relationship/witness clue.

## Movement Integrity

- Every key movement has a time, route, and trace.
- Locked rooms, CCTV, witnesses, keys, and object transfers are consistent.
- The culprit's route is possible but hidden by believable misdirection.
- No suspect is accidentally in two places at once.

## Evidence Hygiene

- No dead evidence.
- No clue solves everything alone too early.
- Every evidence card has surface meaning and true meaning.
- Clue numbering follows reveal pacing, not authoring order.
- Each red herring has a later clearance.

## Ending Quality

- The truth explanation replays the timeline.
- It explains why each innocent suspect looked guilty.
- It identifies the decisive clue and why alternatives fail.
- If there is a second objective, such as finding a missing person or stolen object, it resolves after the culprit reveal.

## Repair Output

When reviewing, report:

1. Blocking fairness issues.
2. Timeline or movement contradictions.
3. Missing proof links.
4. Dead or redundant clues.
5. Strongest fixes with minimal story changes.
